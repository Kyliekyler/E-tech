Rules & Regulations

 

 

INTRODUCTION

  Conduct standards at the School are set forth in writing to give students general notice of prohibited conduct. These rules should be read broadly and are not designed to define prohibited conduct in exhaustive terms.

  Any student to have committed or to have attempted to commit the following misconduct is subject to disciplinary sanctions outlined. It shall not be a defense that a school official, student leader or other person authorized the behavior in question:


Section I - SCHOOL’S GENERAL PARENT-TEACHER’S ASSOCIATION PRESCRIBED UNIFORM

  The wearing of school uniform is not mandatory but we encourage our students to wear the prescribed uniform for their safety. Also, it promotes organization, neatness and time efficiency.

A. Male
  • White polo (with shoulder piping and patch) and gray pants
  • Black shoes with plain white socks
  • School ID
B. Female
  • White collared blouse with patch and scarf
  • Checkered blue pencil-cut skirt
  • Black shoes
C. Haircut
  • For male students, the acceptable haircut shall be at least 2 inches above tge ears and 3 inches above the collar line.
  • Hair color is strictly discouraged.
Rules on Uniform and ID
  1. All students are encouraged to be simple and neat in appearance.
  2. Every student is encouraged to wear the prescribed school uniform at all times during the regular school days.
  3. The blouses of the female students tand the poli of the male students must be properly buttoned. Skirts must be reasonably of decent length.
  4. Undershirts of male students should be in white, not printed nor colored.
  5. The P.E uniform is worn only during P.E classes.
  6. The students’ school I.D. must be visible worn at all times while inside the campus. Lending / borrowing of I.D. is strictly prohibited.
  7. For Male students - No wearing of “hip-hop” pants and other outlandish style of pants, dangling belt, fancy shoes, sandals / slippers, caps, sunglasses, earrings, satanic pendants or bracelets.
  8. For Female students - No wearing of ankle-length skirt, crumpled / printed / colored socks, sandals, fancy shoes, caps, multiple earrings, anklets, make-up / lipstick.
  9. Fancy haircuts, highlighted or dyed hair, tattoos, long / colored nails are not acceptable.

Section II - ATTENDANCE

  Regular and punctual attendance is absolutely necessary. Students must attend flag ceremony at 6:30am every Monday and flag retreat at 2:00pm every Friday.

  1. Tardiness - A student is considered tardy if he/she comes to class 10minutes after the bell has rung.
  2. Cutting classes - A student cuts classes if he/she willfully does not attend in one or more subject(s) but present in some subjects.
  3. Absenteeism
    • A student who has been absent is required to present to the office of Guidance Counselor a written explanation of his absence is signed by his/her parents immediately after the day he/she was absent.
    • A prolonged absence of at least five days (5) caused by illness must be certified with a written statement from a doctor.
    • When a student is absent from classes, he/she is responsible for all matters that were taken up in his/her class during his/her absences and should make all necessary arrangement for missed assignments, lessons and projects with the teacher.
    • The parent/guardian has to personally seek permission for his/her child to leave the school during the class hours to attend social appointments of any kind, together with a written statement signed by the adviser and the guidance counselor.
    • When a student is sick or not feeling well and has to leave the school during class hours, he/she should be fetched by his/her parent/guardian and he/she needs to secure a gate pass from the clinic staff. The gate pass has to be signed by the guidance counselor before presenting to the guard.

Section III - NORMS OF CONDUCT

A. IN THE CAMPUS
  1. All students should give due to respect to the school administrators, teachers, personnel and visitors.
  2. Students are expected to take care of the school properties.
  3. No Student is allowed to loiter around the school premises.
  4. Students are off limits in the faculty rooms and administrative offices unless for valid reasons.
  5. No student is allowed to leave the school campus during class hours without written permission from their advisers / school nurse duly signed by guidance counselor.
  6. Students are encouraged to wear prescribed uniform and I.D. at all times.
  7. Student are discouraged from inviting guests to school for unofficial reasons. Students will be responsible for the behavior of their guests.
  8. Exclusive boy-girl pairings and public display of affections inside the campus is strictly disapproved.
B. INSIDE THE CLASSROOM
  1. Silence must strictly observed in entering and leaving the classroom.
  2. If the teacher is late, the students should review the lessons while waiting. If the teacher is absent, inform immediately the subject Department Head to assign a substitute.
  3. When a teacher or a visitor enters the room, all students should stand erect to greet him/her.
  4. Students should maintain the cleanliness of the classroom. Littering inside the classroom will be dealt with accordingly.
  5. At all times, students should not write anything on the board without the permission of the teacher. The teacher’s desk is exclusively for teacher’s use only. Any damage or loss of school properties inside the room such as the chairs, jalousies, electric bulb, doors, etc. is responsible of the students.
C. IN THE LIBRARY
  1. The Library is open during class days from 7:00am-3:00pm. It is a place for studying and other intellectual activities that require concentration thus, silence must prevail.
  2. A Library card must be secured for students to be able to borrow books in the library.
  3. A student is responsible for the reading materials he/she uses in the Library. In case of losses and damages, the student will be liable for replacing them with the current cost.
D. IN PUBLIC PLACES AND GATHERINGS
  1. Students are expected to behave with kindness and courtesy even outside school.
  2. Refrain from attracting attention by way of boisterous yelling, laughter and giggling. Speak in low voice.

SECTION IV - SCHOOL DISCIPLINE

  Disciplinary measures are employed by the PNHS to correct erring students and for them not to repeat their mistakes, to safeguard the name of the school and to help in the progressive development of the whole studentry.

  1. Warning. The attention of student is formerly called in connection to some offense or violation.
  2. Probation. Habitual misbehavior and unsatisfactory conduct places a student under probation for a specific period of time.
  3. Suspension. The student is deprived from attending classes and school activities for a determined period depending upon the gravity of offense but is asked to stay in the guidance center to serve his/her suspension.
  4. Dropping / Dismissal. A student maybe dropped from the roll if after due process was found guilty of committing a serious major offense. However, if he/she decide to transfer to another school, he/she is supported with a letter of recommendation.

Offense behavior is categorized as either minor or major offenses.

Minor Offenses:
  1. Tardiness
  2. Cutting class
  3. Absenteeism
  4. Improper haircut
  5. Disturbing classes
  6. Littering
  7. Mischief
  8. Forging of excuse letters
  9. Loitering
  10. Lending/Borrowing of I.D.
Major Offenses:
  1. Cheating
  2. Theft
  3. Use of indecent language
  4. Bringing of pornographic pictures
  5. Tampering of public documents
  6. Vandalism - destruction of school properties
  7. Possession and use of deadly weapons such as knives, ice-picks, and hand-made toy guns
  8. Threat / Intimidation / Bullying / Extortion inside or in the vicinity of the school
  9. Possession and use of prohibited drugs, cigarettes, liquor, explosives
  10. Gambling of any kind
  11. Disrespect or defiance of authority
  12. Continuous disregard of school policies / rules
  13. Membership / Participation in exclusive and unauthorized organizations such as fraternities/gangsta
  14. Scandalous display of affection such as kissing, necking, petting
  15. fighting with serious physical injury
  16. Other acts considered malicious or undesirable that create scandal to the moral integrity of the school.

Sanctions

  The school utilizes an educational sanctioning model; hearing officers or panels will make every attempt to provide an educational sanction that will help a student to make better choices in the future. The educational sanction applied will become progressively more demanding if the student repeat violations, demonstrating that learning has not taken place. An accumulation of a variety of violations may result in severe sanctions such as suspension.

COMMISSION OF ANY OF THE MINOR OFFENSES WILL BE DEALT WITH THE FOLLOWING:

First Offense:
 A reprimand by the school authority concerned.

Second Offense:
 The parent/guardian shall be called for appearance in the school and shall be informed of the student’s misconduct and shall conform with the written warning issued to the student. Repeated minor offense is considered a major offense.

NOTE: Executing of minor offense repeatedly is considered as a major offense likewise with various minor offenses.

Third Offense:
 The parent/guardian shall again be summoned for final warning to his/her child. The final warning will constitute the last chance given to the student otherwise; he/she will be forced to leave the school for continuous violation of school rules and regulations.

MAJOR OFFENSE WILL WARRANT A STUDENT OF THE FOLLOWING:

First Offense:
 The parent/guardian shall immediately be called for a case conference. The minimum sanction depending on gravity of the case shall be suspension from the classes. The student, if given another chance will be under probation for a period of time.

Second Offense:
 The student in the presence of his/her parent will be informed that he/she is to be dropped from the roll or may choose to transfer to another school.
